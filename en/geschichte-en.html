The History
of the Raspberry Pi

From a small British idea to a global revolution.

Milestones

A journey through time

2006

The idea is born

Eben Upton and colleagues at Cambridge dream of an affordable computer for everyone. Goal: make programming accessible to all.

2012

Pi 1 Model B launches

100,000 units on day one. A $35 computer stuns the entire world and starts a revolution.

2014

Pi 2 & global education

Quad-core ARM, 1 GB RAM. Schools worldwide integrate the Pi into their curriculum. The Foundation grows rapidly.

2016

Pi Zero – just €5

The size of a stick of gum. Millions of maker projects worldwide follow. Technology becomes democratic.

2019

Pi 4 – desktop ready

USB 3.0, dual 4K output, up to 8 GB RAM. Finally a real desktop replacement for the whole family.

2023

Pi 5 – the most powerful

2–3× faster, PCIe connector, up to 16 GB RAM and custom RP1 chip. The most powerful Pi ever.

today

60+ million connected

Over 60 million units sold in 180+ countries. The Raspberry Pi is more than a product – it's a global movement for education, creativity and open source.
